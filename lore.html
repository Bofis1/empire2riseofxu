The Lore of Xu

They came from the deep faults. They do not negotiate. They do not remember mercy. They are the Xu, and this world was already theirs.— Recovered Resistance Field Report, Author Unknown

Chapter I

The Xu Dominion

Centuries before the events of Empire 2, the Xu were not conquerors — they were architects. Descended from an ancient lineage of scale-plated warriors who built their civilization in the deep fault-lines of the world, the Xu developed technology that blended organic scale-craft with mechanical engineering. Their cities were marvels of neon-lit metal and living stone.

The transformation into conquerors came with the rise of the Xu Supreme Overlord — a military commander who unified the scattered Xu city-states under a single iron banner. What had been a civilization of engineers and warriors became a Dominion: expansionist, authoritarian, and consuming.

We did not choose to resist. We chose to survive. When the Xu rolled through the Outpost gates, every family that fled north became a soldier. Every refugee became a fighter. The Resistance was not organized. It simply refused to stop existing.

— Commander Yelen Ashe, Founding Member of the Free Races Coalition

The Free Races Coalition — Humans, Gnomes, Ogres, Centaurs, Minotaurs, Elves, and Vampires — formed not from ideology but from desperation. The Xu had taken everything: trade routes, cities, farmland, and the ancient ruins that once belonged to everyone. The XU Outpost that players begin in is one of the last strongholds still standing.

Chapter II

The Architects & Ancient Power

Beneath the political conflict runs a deeper mystery. The world is riddled with Ancient Realm structures — ruins that predate the Xu, predate the Free Races, predate written history. Scholars call their builders The Architects.

The Architects left behind technology that neither faction fully understands. Void gems, void weapons, and dimensional anomalies like the Rift Vale are all their legacy. The Xu Supreme Overlord believes controlling Architect technology is the key to total dominion. The Resistance believes it's the only way to stop them.

The Ancient Realm — zone 11 in the game — is a direct entry into Architect territory. What guards it is not of the Xu. It is older. It has been waiting.

Chapter III

The Zones — Path to Xumen

The campaign follows a single line of geographic escalation — from the front lines of the war to the Xu capital itself. Each zone has its own history, its own horror, and its own boss that must fall before you advance.

Level 1+ · Safe Zone
🏚️ XU Outpost
The last Resistance stronghold. Crafting benches, merchants, and the lobby where heroes gather.
Level 5+
⚔️ Patrol Zone
Xu patrol routes. First combat encounter with xu_scouts, soldiers, and their commanding officers.
Level 20+
💀 XU Cemetery
Ancient burial ground repurposed by the Xu. The Lich King has been here for centuries. He was not invited.
Level 15+
🌌 Void Wastes
Dimensional anomalies tear reality here. Void Wraith Prime commands creatures that shouldn't exist.
Level 25+
🏙️ XU Citadel
The Xu military nerve centre. General Vorrakh — traitor, war criminal, fortress commander — awaits at the top.
Level 35+
🌋 Ashlands
Scorched earth. Volcanic activity sustained by Xu geothermal mining. The Inferno Colossus is not a natural creature.
Level 80+
🗡️ Xumen
The Xu capital. Every Xu soldier alive is here. The Supreme Overlord commands from the central spire.
Level 100+ · Endgame
☠️ The Necropolis
It rains. Lightning kills you. The Bone Colossus — 1,400,000 HP, four phases, void-crack skull the size of a building — waits at the centre.
Chapter IV

The 16 Bosses

Every zone has a guardian. Some were created by the Xu. Some are older than the Xu. Some are things no one created on purpose.

☠️
The Bone Colossus
Necropolis · 1,400,000 HP

A void-crack entity that assembled itself from the remains of everyone who fell in the Necropolis war. Four phases. The fourth phase involves the sky joining the fight. No one has confirmed how it began existing.

🔺
The Apex Pyramid
Xumen Fortress · Level 100+

A Xu military spacecraft repurposed as a fortress AI. Orbital cannon arrays, tractor beams, and a rotating hull make it unkillable from range. The Xu Supreme Overlord built it as a last resort. It became a first resort.

🐲
Vaeltharax The Undying
Dragon Lair · 5 Phases

She has been killed fourteen times. She comes back. The Dragon Lair is littered with the armour of adventurers who thought the fifth phase was the last one. It is not. She enters a new phase when "dead." Her fifth phase unlocks abilities she wasn't supposed to have.

🌀
The Rift Sovereign
Rift Vale · Dimensional Anomaly

The Rift Vale is a zone where dimensional collapse is ongoing. The Rift Sovereign is not a creature that entered through the rift — it IS the rift. Destroying it collapses the anomaly. The world is not sure this is safe.

⚔️
General Vorrakh
XU Citadel · The Traitor General

Vorrakh was once a Free Races general who defected to the Xu Dominion in exchange for promises of power. He got what he was promised. The Citadel garrison worships him. The Resistance has a word for him in every language they speak. None of them are kind.

🧙
The Lich King
Cemetery · Centuries Dead

Not a Xu creation. The Lich King predates the Xu by four centuries. He was ruling the Cemetery before the Xu arrived, and he will still be there after. He permits the Xu to operate nearby because they provide a steady stream of the newly dead. He finds this convenient.

🤖
XU Supreme Overlord
Xumen · The Command Chain

The architect of the Xu Dominion. Not a warlord. Not a conqueror. An administrator. The Supreme Overlord built the Dominion from bureaucratic precision — every zone, every patrol route, every slave camp was authorized through proper channels. He is the most dangerous kind of enemy: an organized one.

🐲
Stonewyrm Colossus
Wyvern Wastes · Level 80+

Ancient. Massive. The Wyvern Wastes were its territory long before either faction arrived. It does not care about the war. It cares about the noise. The Xu brought the noise. It will flatten both sides with equal indifference.

Chapter V

The Races of the Coalition

The Free Races Coalition was not designed — it was collided into existence. Eight species that had spent centuries trading, competing, and occasionally warring with each other found themselves united by a single existential threat.

The Xu themselves can choose to fight against their own Dominion — former soldiers, escaped prisoners, and ideological dissidents who believe the Overlord has led their civilization to ruin. Playing as a Xu in the Resistance is considered the hardest alignment in the game. Every soldier you fight used to be your people.

The Vampires of the Deep Houses joined last. For centuries they had maintained careful neutrality with the Xu Dominion, trading void-gems and arcane knowledge in exchange for being left alone. When the Xu broke that agreement and raided the Deep Houses, the Vampires did not forgive. They joined the Coalition not as allies — as instruments of a very specific revenge.

The Gnomes built the weapons. The Ogres carried them. The Centaurs delivered them. The Elves aimed them. The Humans convinced everyone this was worth doing. And the Vampires reminded everyone what happens when it isn't.

— Anonymous Coalition Historian, "The Unlikely Resistance"
